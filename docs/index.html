Governed Generative AI: Organizational Architecture for Regulated Work

This page is the Stage 2 entry point for the Governance-First AI training suite: governed generative AI applied to specific regulated domains. At this stage, governance is not a memo. It is operational architecture: gates, logs, manifests, reviewer sign-off, refusal posture, traceability, and evidence bundles. The Architecture Patterns (Stage 2)

In Stage 2, governance is operational: it is built into how work is produced, reviewed, and released. These patterns are shared across all domains, even though the content differs.

  • Scope contract: define task, audience, allowed inputs, forbidden outputs, and stop-conditions when facts are missing.
  • Separation of roles: generation ≠ verification ≠ approval (even if one person performs multiple roles, the structure stays explicit).
  • Evidence bundle: every run produces artifacts: run manifest, prompts log, assumptions register, risk log, and deliverables folder.
  • Review packet: the output is packaged for supervision (what was asked, what was assumed, what is unknown, what must be verified).
  • Refusal posture: the system must refuse disallowed work (e.g., fabricate citations, invent legal authority, imply investment advice).
  • Release gates: no external use without named reviewer sign-off; claims must be traceable to evidence.
Why this matters for MBA/MFin cohorts: the discipline trains students to produce outputs that can survive real organizational constraints: compliance review, auditability, client confidentiality, and professional accountability.
